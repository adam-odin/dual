Use Disk Manager to partition space on your laptop for your Ubuntu

The first thing you need to do is make sure you have space for installing ubuntu and managing everything side by side with windows.

  • UEFI start up boot
  • C: Your pc or Solid State Disk
  • Recovery for Windows
  • D: Your Hard Disk Drive
  • Any virtual machines

There are three main parts to think about: /root /home and RAM

SSD for /root

HDD for /home

I really like the blog here: and the proposed solution to use HDD for home and SSD for main install. Ideally if you have a lot of space in SSD then yuo can have it all in SSD and just use HDD for backups and large data files.

What to do if you don't have much contiguous space in your drives?

If you find you don't have much space in your SSD (C:)

  • move documents to hard disk drive
  • defragmenter
