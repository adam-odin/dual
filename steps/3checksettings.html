As I am revisiting this for writing up this section, I remember this was more complicated than it first appeared - but not insurmountable!

I am making another ordered list here because it is important to get each of these steps right!

*And by way of warning, there is quite a lot of restarting initialy so bear with it *

  1. Change your restart to Safe Mode
  2. Go to your EUFI/BIOS settings menu by restarting your computer and pressing F2 immediately upon restart then selecting BIOS setup
  3. Change your UEFI setting from "RAID On" to "AHCI"
  4. Save and Exit and continue to verify that windows still reboots correctly in safe mode
  5. Change your restart to Safe Mode
  6. Change your UEFI setting from "RAID On" to "AHCI"
  7. Change your UEFI setting from "RAID On" to "AHCI"
  8. Consider changing your Secure Boot to "Disabled"
  9. You might need to change your Start Override values to zero however I wouldn't bother with this is you don't need to

You will get plenty of severe looking warnings along the way so we'll walk through each step in a little more detail so you have confidence to forge ahead despite the warnings - and hopefully your confidence is justified, or at least you can easily fix it if you find something goes wrong.

Change UEFI Settings

The UEFI settings control how your machine starts up:
Go to UEFI / BIOS settings menu Restart your laptop. Immediately upon starting up hit F2 (This key varies by manufacturer but is often one of the following: Esc, Delete, F1, F2, F10, F11, or F12.

Advanced Startup (Windows Method): If you have Windows installed, you can also access UEFI settings through the operating system. Go to Settings > Update & Security > Recovery, and under Advanced startup, click Restart now. Then select Troubleshoot > Advanced Options > UEFI Firmware Settings, and click Restart
