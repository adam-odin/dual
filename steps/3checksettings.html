Check UEFI Settings

The UEFI settings control how your machine starts up: I am making another ordered list here because it is important to get each of these steps right!

  1. Change your UEFI setting from "RAID On" to "AHCI"
  2. Change your restart to Safe Mode
  3. Consider changing your Secure Boot to "Disabled"
  4. You might need to change your Start Override values to zero however I wouldn't bother with this is you don't need to

You will get plenty of severe looking warnings along the way so we'll walk through each step in a little more detail so you have confidence to forge ahead despite the warnings - and hopefully your confidence is justified, or at least we can find an easy fix if it goes wrong.
