I have just begun learning about web development, enrolled on The Odin Project and I am working through the exercises.

The Odin Project supports ubuntu and recommends various methods for getting access to linux on your machine, including dual-booting, using a virtual machine or using WSL. My interpretation is: ultimately 100% linux (possibly 5% for things windows is best for like video editing, gaming, maybe)

I am committed to learning and completing The Odin Project so I decide to commit to dual boot on my machine. I backed up my files and prepared for some unpleasantness in pursuit of programming knowledge.

This seems to be recommended but only once people have gotten comfortable with using linux in a more tentative / less committal way first, namely through using a virtual machine or WSL. Setting up a virtual machine also requires one to set aside storage space, albeit with somewhat less control over whether to save your file in SSD or HDD. Who knows, perhaps with this guide, more Odin Project people will be encouraged to go straight for dual boot and ultimately save themselves some time in the progress. I am glad I did and it would be great if this can (hopefully safely!) help others to do the same.

Several of the website references I have used here were suggested by them as part of the answers to my questions. I have also found I have struggled more, for various reasons, when testing out other chatbots including co-pilot in microsoft edge or on mobile app, OpenAI's chatgpt, Claude, merlin AI (aggregator of AI chatbots) and others. Let's be honest, all of these LLMs present errors sooner or later and they can be damaging - without any harm coming to the chatbots themselves - so having as much control over verifying what AI chatbots suggest to us makes sense. Having sources used in formulating an answer and web links provided is desirable and provide some reassurance or at least the opportunity to check the quality (validity and reliability) of where these answers are coming from and should help identify or even eliminate some of the more fictitions hallucinations. There is definitely a word of caution merited when it comes to using any AI chatbots because they can make some seemingly small suggestions that have far-reaching consequences despite being entirely unnecessary in the first place, so it is better to verify before following any AI chatbot instructions!
In fact there are often so many complicated answers provided (I guess depending on the assumed knowledge of the "typical" person asking the questions) that it makes it all the more important to simply and accurately lay out the steps and provide a working solution with readily verifiable authenticity.
